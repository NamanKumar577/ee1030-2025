\section*{Question}
Find the equation of the circle passing through $(0, 0)$ and making intercepts $a$ and $b$ on the coordinate axes.


\section*{Solution}

Let:
\begin{align}
\vec{x_1} = \myvec{0 \\ 0},\ \vec{x_2} = \myvec{a \\ 0},\ \vec{x_3} = \myvec{0 \\ b}
\end{align}

We use the general matrix form of a circle:


\begin{align}
\myvec{2x_1 & 2x_2 & 2x_3\\
1 & 1 & 1}^T \myvec{\vec{u} \\ f} = - \myvec{\norm{x_1}^2 \\ \norm{x_2}^2 \\ \norm{x_3}^2}
\end{align}


\begin{align}
\myvec{
2x_1^T & 1 \\
2x_2^T & 1 \\
2x_3^T & 1
}
\myvec{
\vec{u} \\
f
}
=
- \myvec{
\norm{x_1}^2 \\
\norm{x_2}^2 \\
\norm{x_3}^2
}
\end{align}


Substituting the values:

\begin{align}
\myvec{
0 & 0 & 1 \\
2a & 0 & 1 \\
0 & 2b & 1
}
\myvec{
u_1 \\
u_2 \\
f
}
=
- \myvec{
0 \\
a^2 \\
b^2
}
\end{align}


Solving the system:

\begin{align*}
f &= 0 \\
2a u_1 + f &= -a^2 \Rightarrow u_1 = -\frac{a}{2} \\
2b u_2 + f &= -b^2 \Rightarrow u_2 = -\frac{b}{2}
\end{align*}

\subsection*{Final Result}

\begin{align}
\vec{u} = \myvec{ -\frac{a}{2} \\ -\frac{b}{2} }, \quad f = 0
\end{align}


So the equation of the circle becomes:


\begin{align}
x^2 + y^2 + ax + by = 0
\end{align}
