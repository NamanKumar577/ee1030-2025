\textbf{Question:}  
If $|\vec{a}\times \vec{b}|^2 + (\vec{a}^T \vec{b})^2 = 144$ and $||\vec{a}|| = 4$, then $||\vec{b}||$ is equal to \underline{\hspace{2cm}}\\

\textbf{Solution:}  

We know that
\begin{align}
|\vec{a}\times \vec{b}|^2 + (\vec{a}^T \vec{b})^2
&= ||\vec{a}||^2||\vec{b}||^2
\end{align}

Given :
\begin{align}
|\vec{a}\times \vec{b}|^2 + (\vec{a}^T \vec{b})^2 &= 144, \\
||\vec{a}|| &= 4, \\
144 &= ||\vec{a}||^2||\vec{b}||^2 \\
144 &= 4^2||\vec{b}||^2 \\
144 &= 16||\vec{b}||^2 \\
||\vec{b}||^2 &= \frac{144}{16} = 9 \\
||\vec{b}|| &= 3.
\end{align}
